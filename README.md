## 📌 Abstract

This project investigates urban flooding and its impact on vulnerable communities in Mumbai through two major field engagements:

1. **NGO Interaction** — A visit to [YUVA (Youth for Unity and Voluntary Action)](https://yuvaindia.org/) in Kurla, Mumbai, to understand the structural and environmental causes of urban flooding.
2. **Community Survey** — A structured survey across multiple wards of Mumbai to collect primary data on flood frequency, severity, financial losses, and preparedness.

Key findings indicate that flooding in Mumbai is intensified by rapid urbanization, poor stormwater drainage, loss of wetlands and mangroves, and unplanned construction — disproportionately affecting informal, low-income settlements.

## 📊 Key Findings

- **Flooding has become more frequent** — multiple times per year in several wards, with water levels reaching knee to waist height.
- **Urbanization amplifies risk** — concretization, loss of wetlands, and shrinking open spaces increase surface runoff dramatically.
- **Informal settlements are most vulnerable** — low-lying and reclaimed areas experience prolonged water stagnation and higher economic losses.
- **Significant socio-economic impact** — flooding disrupts transport, employment, education, electricity, and public health.
- **Preparedness gaps remain** — early warning systems exist but community-level resilience is inconsistent.

---

## 💡 Recommendations

- Expand flood risk awareness programs to more vulnerable wards
- Conduct ward-wise GIS-based flood vulnerability mapping
- Implement stricter land-use regulations in high-risk zones
- Protect wetlands, mangroves, and natural water retention zones
- Integrate data science tools for continuous flood pattern monitoring
- Strengthen collaboration between NGOs, municipal authorities, and communities
